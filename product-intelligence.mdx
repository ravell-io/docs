Product Intelligence analyzes data from your connected sources — Slack, Linear, Intercom — and builds a **product graph** of problems, feature requests, and topics your customers keep mentioning. Instead of manually triaging feedback, you get a ranked dashboard of what matters most.

---

## Overview dashboard

The dashboard shows a curated summary of your product landscape:

- **Top problems** — Ranked by a priority score that combines evidence count, source diversity, recency, and velocity. Each problem shows its trend (rising, stable, or declining) and whether it has a response.
- **Emerging issues** — Blind spots where evidence is surging or a coverage gap exists. These are issues that might not rank highly yet but are changing fast.
- **Health metrics** — Aggregate stats including total entities, assertions, and mean confidence across your product graph.

You can **dismiss** irrelevant problems from the ranked list to keep the dashboard focused.

---

## Entity explorer

Click **Browse all** from the dashboard to open the entity explorer — a searchable, filterable view of everything in your product graph.

### Browsing entities

- **Search** by name across all entities
- **Filter by type** — Problems, Feature requests, or Topics
- **Sort** by mention count or name
- Select any entity to see its full detail in the side panel

### Entity detail

Each entity shows:

- **Evidence** — Individual assertions extracted from your sources, each with the source content, source type, confidence score, and date
- **Connections** — Relationships to other entities (e.g., a problem is related to a feature request), with relationship type and confidence

Entities with a confidence below 60% display a **low-confidence badge** to indicate the extraction is uncertain.

## Weekly brief

The weekly brief is an automated Slack digest that delivers your top problems and trends on a recurring schedule.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Product Intelligence">
    Navigate to **Product Intelligence** from the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up the brief">
    Click **Set up weekly brief** in the onboarding card (or the brief section if you've already explored the dashboard).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a Slack channel">
    Select the channel where you want the brief delivered.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the schedule">
    Pick the day of the week, time, and timezone. The default is Monday morning.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. You can also **Send test** to preview the brief immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once active, you can **pause**, **resume**, or **edit** the brief at any time from the dashboard.

## Getting started

Product Intelligence activates automatically once you connect sources. The onboarding flow guides you through three stages:

1. **Connect your tools** — Link Slack, Linear, or Intercom so Ravell has data to analyze.
2. **Building your intelligence** — Ravell extracts entities and builds the product graph. This happens in the background.
3. **Insights ready** — Once extraction completes, you see how many problems were found and can set up the weekly brief.

<Tip>
The more sources you connect, the richer your product graph becomes. Problems mentioned across multiple sources score higher in the rankings.
</Tip>

## Key concepts

| Concept | What it means |
|---------|---------------|
| **Entity** | A product concept — problem, feature request, or topic — extracted from your connected sources |
| **Assertion** | A single piece of evidence linking a source mention to an entity, with a confidence score |
| **Priority score** | A composite score ranking problems by evidence count, source diversity, recency, and velocity |
| **Velocity** | Whether mentions of a problem are rising, steady, or declining over time |
| **Blind spot** | An emerging issue where evidence is changing significantly — a new surge or coverage gap |
| **Confidence** | A 0–100% score indicating how certain the extraction is. Below 60% triggers a visual indicator |
| **Source diversity** | How many distinct source types (Slack, Linear, Intercom) mention a problem |
