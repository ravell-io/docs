Product intelligence gives you a ranked, evidence-backed view of what your customers care about most — without manually reading through tickets, Slack threads, and CRM notes. Ravell continuously analyzes your connected sources and surfaces the issues that matter.

---

## What is product intelligence?

Product intelligence is a structured layer that sits on top of Ravell's knowledge graph. While [Asks](/asks) let you pull answers on demand, product intelligence **pushes** insights to you — automatically extracting entities from your data and ranking them by importance.

### Why it matters

Most teams know they have product feedback scattered across tools. The problem is turning that noise into signal. Product intelligence does the heavy lifting: it reads your Intercom conversations, Linear issues, Slack threads, Notion pages, and Attio records, then extracts and ranks what matters.

---

## What Ravell tracks

Ravell extracts three types of entities from your connected sources:

| Entity type | What it is | Example |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| **Problem** | A customer pain point, bug, or issue | "Checkout flow times out on mobile" |
| **Feature request** | Something customers are asking for | "Add bulk export for invoices" |
| **Topic** | A broader theme or area of interest | "Mobile experience" |

Each entity is backed by **evidence** — the specific mentions, conversations, and issues where Ravell found it. You can always click through to see exactly where a claim comes from.

## The overview

Open **Product Intelligence** in the sidebar to see a ranked view of what matters most across your connected sources.

### Top problems

The overview shows a ranked table of the most pressing problems, ordered by priority score. Each row includes:

- **Rank** — Position based on a composite of evidence volume, source diversity, recency, and confidence
- **Problem name** — A normalized label Ravell extracted from your data
- **Priority score** — A weighted score reflecting how urgent the problem is
- **Evidence count** — How many mentions Ravell found across your sources
- **Trend** — Whether mentions are rising, steady, or declining over time
- **Sources** — How many different tools (Linear, Intercom, Slack, etc.) mention this problem
- **Status** — Whether your team has already responded to this issue or not

### Emerging issues

Below the top problems, Ravell highlights **emerging issues** — blind spots your team may not be tracking yet. These are problems or themes that are growing in mentions but haven't been addressed. Each shows a severity score and the number of sources mentioning it.

### Health metrics

A summary bar shows overall coverage: total entities tracked, total evidence collected, and mean confidence across your data.

## Exploring entities

Click **Entities** to browse the full list of everything Ravell has extracted. You can:

- **Filter by type** — Switch between All, Problems, Feature Requests, and Topics
- **Search** — Find specific entities by name
- **Sort** — Order by mention count or alphabetically

Click any entity to see its detail view:

- **Evidence** — Every mention, sorted by confidence. Each shows the source content, source type (Linear, Intercom, Slack, etc.), confidence percentage, and date.
- **Connections** — Relationships to other entities (e.g., a problem that "causes" another problem, or a feature request "related to" a topic). These connections are built from Ravell's knowledge graph.

---

## Dismissing irrelevant items

Not every extracted problem is relevant. If Ravell surfaces something that doesn't matter to your team, click the dismiss button (the X icon) on any problem row. Dismissed items:

- Disappear from your ranked view immediately
- Help Ravell learn what matters to your team over time
- Can still be found in the full entity explorer if needed

<Tip>
Dismissing a few irrelevant items early on helps Ravell learn your team's priorities faster.
</Tip>

## Weekly brief

Set up a recurring Slack digest to get a summary of your top product intelligence delivered to a channel each week.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the weekly brief section">
    Scroll to the bottom of the **Product Intelligence** overview and find the weekly brief card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a Slack channel">
    Select the channel where you want the digest delivered.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a schedule">
    Choose a day (Monday through Friday), time, and timezone. The default is Monday at 9:00 AM UTC.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable the brief">
    Click **Enable**. You can send a test message first to preview the format.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once enabled, you can pause, resume, or change the schedule anytime. The brief summarizes your top problems, trends, and any new emerging issues since the last digest.

---

## How it builds over time

Product intelligence starts working as soon as you connect your first source, but it gets better over time:

- **More sources** mean more evidence and higher confidence scores
- **Dismissals** teach Ravell what's relevant to your team
- **New data** from ongoing conversations, issues, and tickets keeps the rankings fresh

When you first connect sources, you'll see an onboarding card showing the progress of the initial analysis. Once complete, it tells you how many problems Ravell found in your data.

## Tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="When should I check product intelligence?">
    Check the overview weekly or before planning sessions. It's designed to give you a quick pulse on what customers care about without reading every ticket.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How is this different from Asks?">
    Asks are on-demand — you ask a question and get an answer. Product intelligence is proactive — Ravell continuously extracts and ranks entities without you asking. Use Asks for specific questions; use product intelligence for a broad view of what matters.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I combine product intelligence with agents?">
    Yes. Agent templates like **Feature request clustering** and **Churn signals** query the same underlying data. Product intelligence gives you the ranked view; agents give you scheduled reports on specific topics.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if I disagree with a ranking?">
    Dismiss items that aren't relevant. Over time, Ravell learns your team's priorities. You can also use the entity explorer to drill into the evidence and judge for yourself.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
