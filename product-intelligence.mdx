## What is Product intelligence?

**Product intelligence** is Ravell's automatic analysis layer. It continuously processes data from your connected sources — Linear, Intercom, Slack, Notion, and Attio — to identify customer problems, feature requests, and topics without you asking a question.

Open **Product intelligence** from the sidebar to see the dashboard.

**Why it matters:** Instead of manually sifting through tickets, conversations, and documents, Ravell surfaces what matters: the top problems your customers face, emerging issues nobody has responded to, and how evidence is trending over time. You get a prioritized view of your product's reality, updated as new data flows in.

---

## Overview dashboard

The dashboard shows four metric cards at the top:

| Metric | What it shows |
|--------|---------------|
| **Problems** | Customer pain points extracted from your sources. Shows how many of the top problems have no team response yet. |
| **Emerging issues** | Rising problems that nobody has responded to. Includes the highest severity score when issues are detected. |
| **Entities** | Total count of problems, feature requests, and topics tracked across all sources, plus the number of relationships between them. |
| **This week** | New evidence collected from your integrations in the last 7 days, with a 30-day comparison. |

Below the metrics, you'll see two sections:

### Top problems

A ranked table of the most significant customer problems. Each row shows:

- **Rank** — Position based on priority score.
- **Problem** — The problem name, identified by Ravell from your sources.
- **Score** — A priority score based on evidence count, source diversity, confidence, and recency.
- **Evidence** — Number of mentions across your connected sources.
- **Sources** — How many different source types (Slack, Linear, Intercom, etc.) mention this problem. Higher diversity means stronger signal.
- **Status** — Whether your team has responded to this problem or not.

### Emerging issues

Cards showing problems where evidence is rising but your team hasn't responded yet. Each card displays the issue name, type, severity score, and how many source types it appears in.

These are your blind spots — problems gaining traction that might slip through the cracks without proactive attention.

## Entity explorer

Click **Browse all** on the dashboard to open the entity explorer. This is a searchable, filterable catalog of everything Ravell has identified across your sources.

### Entity types

Ravell classifies entities into three categories:

- **Problems** — Customer pain points and issues.
- **Feature requests** — Things customers or team members have asked for.
- **Topics** — General themes or areas of discussion.

### Browsing and filtering

- **Search** — Find entities by name.
- **Filter by type** — Use the tabs (All, Problems, Requests, Topics) to narrow the list.
- **Sort** — Sort by mentions (default, most mentioned first) or alphabetically by name.
- **Filter by mentions** — Show all entities, only those with mentions, or only those without mentions.

### Entity detail view

Select an entity to see its full detail:

- **Evidence** — All sources where this entity was mentioned, ranked by confidence. Each piece of evidence shows the source content, source type, confidence percentage, and date.
- **Connections** — How this entity relates to other problems, feature requests, and topics. Relationships have types (e.g. "related to", "caused by") and confidence scores.

<Tip>
Low-confidence entities display a badge indicating that Ravell isn't certain this is a real product issue yet. As more evidence appears, confidence increases.
</Tip>

## Weekly brief

The weekly brief delivers a summary of your product intelligence to a Slack channel on a schedule. It includes ranked problems, emerging issues, and trends.

<Note>
The weekly brief requires a connected Slack integration. If Slack is not connected, the brief option won't appear on the dashboard.
</Note>

### Set up a weekly brief

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Product intelligence">
    Navigate to **Product intelligence** from the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Set up on the Weekly Brief card">
    The card appears below the dashboard title. Click **Set up** to configure.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a Slack channel">
    Choose the channel where you want the brief delivered. The Ravell bot must be invited to the channel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable">
    Click **Enable**. The brief sends every Monday at 9am UTC by default.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Edit or pause

Click the Weekly Brief card to open the editor. From here you can:

- **Change the channel** — Select a different Slack channel.
- **Change the schedule** — Pick a different day (Monday–Friday), time, and timezone.
- **Send a test** — Deliver a test brief to the selected channel immediately.
- **Pause** — Stop the brief from running. Resume any time.

## How it works

Product intelligence is built on Ravell's [knowledge graph](/knowledge-graph). As documents are indexed from your sources, Ravell:

1. **Extracts assertions** — Identifies claims, complaints, requests, and topics from source content.
2. **Resolves entities** — Groups related assertions into unified entities, even when different sources use different names.
3. **Scores and ranks** — Calculates priority scores based on evidence count, source diversity, confidence, recency, and velocity trends.
4. **Detects blind spots** — Flags entities where evidence is rising but no team response exists.

The system improves as you connect more sources and accumulate more data. More evidence means higher-confidence rankings and better blind spot detection.
