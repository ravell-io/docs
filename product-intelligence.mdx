## What is product intelligence?

Product intelligence is a layer that sits on top of Ravell's [knowledge graph](/knowledge-graph). It automatically extracts **product entities** — problems, feature requests, and topics — from your connected sources, scores them by priority, and surfaces what matters most.

Instead of asking individual questions, product intelligence continuously analyzes your Linear issues, Intercom conversations, Slack messages, Notion pages, and Attio data to build a structured view of what your customers are experiencing and what your team should focus on.

**Why it matters:** You get a living, evidence-backed picture of your product's top problems and gaps — without manually triaging feedback across tools.

---

## How it works

When Ravell indexes a document from any connected source, it runs an extraction pipeline that:

1. **Identifies product entities** — problems, feature requests, and topics mentioned in the document.
2. **Creates assertions** — evidence-backed claims linking entities to source documents (e.g., "slow dashboard loading" is evidenced by 3 Intercom conversations and 2 Linear issues).
3. **Scores and ranks** — each problem gets a composite priority score based on evidence volume, confidence, recency, source diversity, and engagement.
4. **Detects patterns** — rising trends, blind spots, and improving problems are surfaced automatically.

The more sources you connect, the richer the intelligence. A problem mentioned in Intercom conversations, Linear issues, and Slack threads scores higher than one seen in a single source.

---

## Entity types

Product intelligence tracks three types of entities:

| Entity type | What it represents | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem** | A recurring customer pain point or product deficiency | "Slow dashboard loading for large datasets" |
| **Feature request** | A proposed product improvement that doesn't exist yet | "Bulk CSV export for analytics" |
| **Topic** | A broad domain that groups related problems and requests | "Analytics dashboard", "Billing and payments" |

Entities are extracted from your documents using both structured metadata (e.g., Linear issue labels) and AI analysis of document content. Ravell resolves duplicates across sources — the same problem described differently in Slack and Intercom maps to a single entity.

---

## Scored problems

Every problem entity receives a **priority score** based on five weighted factors:

| Factor | What it measures |
|---|---|
| **Evidence** | How many documents mention this problem |
| **Confidence** | How reliably the problem was identified across sources |
| **Recency** | How recently the problem was mentioned (recent evidence weighs more) |
| **Source diversity** | How many different tools surface this problem (cross-source signals are stronger) |
| **Engagement** | How your team interacts with the entity (reviewing, dismissing, or requesting more detail) |

Problems with high scores across multiple factors are the ones most likely to deserve your attention.

## Blind spots

Blind spots are **rising problems with no planned response**. Ravell identifies these by looking for problems that:

- Have increasing evidence over the last two weeks compared to the prior baseline
- Have no associated feature request addressing them

These are the gaps in your roadmap — problems that are getting worse but aren't being worked on. Each blind spot includes a severity score based on how fast evidence is growing.

## Weekly brief

The **weekly brief** is an automated digest delivered to a Slack channel. It summarizes the most important product intelligence insights for your team.

Each brief includes:

- **Rising problems** — issues with increasing velocity, including evidence counts and source diversity
- **Blind spots** — uncovered rising problems with severity scores
- **Improving problems** — issues where evidence decreased after a feature was shipped
- **Changes since last brief** — new entries, rank changes, and problems that dropped off the list
- **Possible duplicates** — entities that may refer to the same thing, with similarity scores

### Set up the weekly brief

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open settings">
    Go to **Settings** → **Product Intelligence** in your workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable the brief">
    Turn on the weekly brief and select the Slack channel where it should be delivered.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure schedule">
    The default schedule is every Monday at 9:00 AM. Adjust the day, time, and timezone to match your team's rhythm.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your first brief">
    The brief runs at the next scheduled time. You can also trigger it manually to see an immediate preview.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Reply to a brief in Slack to ask follow-up questions. Ravell responds in the thread with additional context about the problems and trends mentioned.
</Tip>

## Entity management

### Reviewing entities

When you interact with product intelligence, Ravell learns from your behavior to improve future prioritization:

- **Tell me more** on a brief item signals interest and boosts the entity's priority
- **Mark reviewed** acknowledges you've seen the entity
- **Dismiss** reduces the entity's priority in future rankings

### Merging duplicates

Ravell suggests merges when two entities appear to describe the same thing (e.g., "checkout timeout errors" and "payment page timeouts"). You can:

- **Accept the merge** — the entities are combined, and their evidence is unified
- **Undo a merge** — if a merge was incorrect, reverse it to restore both entities

## Querying product intelligence

You can access product intelligence insights through multiple surfaces:

### In Slack

Ask the Slack Agent questions like:

- "What are the top problems right now?"
- "Are there any blind spots in our roadmap?"
- "Tell me more about the dashboard performance problem"
- "Search for entities related to billing"

The Slack Agent uses product intelligence data to give you scored, evidence-backed answers.

### Via the API

If you use Ravell's API, product intelligence data is available through the knowledge endpoints. You can query for scored problems, blind spots, and graph health programmatically.

---

## How the graph improves

Product intelligence gets smarter as your workspace grows:

- **More documents** mean more evidence for each entity, improving confidence and scoring accuracy
- **More sources** increase source diversity, making cross-tool patterns visible
- **Your interactions** — reviewing, dismissing, and merging entities — train the system on what matters to your team
- **Time** — the system tracks velocity, so it can distinguish rising problems from stable ones and detect when shipped features reduce reported issues
