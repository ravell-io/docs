## Overview

Product intelligence gives you a structured view of the problems, features, and decisions in your product — built automatically from your connected sources. Instead of manually tagging and categorizing, Ravell extracts product entities and relationships from Linear issues, Intercom conversations, Slack discussions, and Notion docs.

<Note>Product intelligence is currently available to select workspaces. Contact your Ravell admin if you don't see it in your sidebar.</Note>

---

## What it tracks

Ravell builds a product graph with four entity types:

| Entity type | What it represents | Example |
|-------------|-------------------|---------|
| **Problem** | A customer pain point or issue | "Users can't export reports to CSV" |
| **Feature** | A product capability or planned work | "CSV export for reports" |
| **Decision** | A product decision or prioritization call | "Defer CSV export to Q3" |
| **Product area** | A high-level area of the product | "Reporting" |

These entities are connected by relationships — a problem affects a customer, a feature addresses a problem, a decision deprioritizes a feature, and features belong to product areas.

---

## Problems

The **Problems** view shows customer pain points ranked by priority. Each problem is scored based on:

- **Evidence count** — how many sources mention this problem
- **Customer impact** — how many customers are affected
- **Recency** — when the problem was last reported
- **Temporal trends** — whether mentions are increasing or decreasing

Problems with more evidence from more customers, reported more recently, rank higher.

---

## Blind spots

**Blind spots** are problems that have no coverage — no feature addressing them, no decision resolving them. These are the gaps your team may not be aware of.

Use blind spots to find:

- Customer pain points with no planned work
- Recurring complaints that haven't been triaged
- Emerging issues that haven't been discussed yet

---

## Graph health

The **Health** view shows aggregate metrics about your product graph:

- Entity counts by type
- Assertion coverage (how well-evidenced your entities are)
- Data staleness indicators

Use this to understand how complete your product picture is and whether you need to connect more sources.

---

## Entity explorer

Browse all entities in the product graph. You can filter by type, search by name, and drill into any entity to see:

- **Assertions** — the specific evidence supporting this entity, with confidence scores and source links
- **Relationships** — how this entity connects to other problems, features, decisions, and product areas

---

## Weekly product brief

The product brief is a weekly digest delivered to Slack that summarizes what changed in your product landscape. It includes:

- **Top problems** — highest-priority customer pain points
- **New blind spots** — problems without coverage since the last brief
- **Velocity tracking** — what shipped and what slipped
- **Merge suggestions** — duplicate entities that should be combined

### Set up the brief

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open product intelligence">
    Go to **Product Intelligence** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable the brief">
    Click **Enable brief** and choose a Slack channel for delivery.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure schedule">
    Set your preferred day, time, and timezone. The default is weekly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send a test">
    Click **Test brief** to send a preview to the selected channel immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Interactive actions

When the brief is delivered to Slack, it includes interactive buttons:

- **Tell me more** — get a deeper explanation of a specific problem or entity
- **Mark reviewed** — accept all pending assertions for an entity
- **Merge entities** — combine duplicate entities directly from the brief

## How extraction works

<Accordion title="Behind the scenes">
Ravell automatically extracts product entities and relationships from your indexed documents using a combination of:

- **LLM-based extraction** — analyzes document content to identify problems, features, decisions, and their relationships
- **Metadata extraction** — uses structured data (labels, tags, statuses) to create assertions
- **Deduplication** — matches new extractions against existing entities to avoid duplicates
- **Confidence scoring** — each assertion has a confidence score. High-confidence assertions are promoted automatically; lower-confidence ones are held for review.

The graph gets richer over time as more documents are indexed and more relationships are discovered.
</Accordion>
