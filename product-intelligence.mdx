Product Intelligence gives you a real-time dashboard built from Ravell's [knowledge graph](/knowledge-graph). Instead of asking questions, you get a continuously updated view of what's happening across your product — ranked problems, blind spots, entity relationships, and a weekly brief delivered to Slack.

---

## Overview dashboard

The overview is your starting point. It shows four metric cards at the top:

| Metric | What it shows |
|--------|--------------|
| **Problems** | Total problems detected, plus how many lack a corresponding feature |
| **Blind spots** | Areas with rising or unaddressed signals, ranked by severity |
| **Entities** | Total entities (problems, features, decisions, areas) and relationships in the graph |
| **This week** | New assertions in the last 7 days, with a 30-day comparison |

Below the metrics, you see two ranked lists:

- **Top problems** — A table of the highest-priority problems, scored by evidence count, source diversity, confidence, and recency. Each row shows whether the problem has a corresponding feature or not.
- **Blind spots** — Cards highlighting areas where Ravell detected signals you may be missing, with severity scores and source breakdowns.

---

## Entity explorer

Click **Browse all** from the overview to open the entity explorer. This is a searchable, filterable list of every entity Ravell has extracted from your sources.

### Entity types

| Type | Description |
|------|------------|
| **Problem** | A pain point, bug, or issue mentioned across sources |
| **Feature** | A capability, enhancement, or shipped feature |
| **Decision** | A product or engineering decision referenced in discussions |
| **Product area** | A broader theme or domain grouping related entities |

### Searching and filtering

Use the search bar to find entities by name. Switch between tabs to filter by type: All, Problems, Features, Decisions, or Areas. The list loads more results as you scroll.

### Entity detail

Select an entity to see its detail panel:

- **Evidence** — Every assertion (mention) from your sources, ranked by confidence. Each assertion shows the source type, source title, content snippet, confidence percentage, and when it was last seen.
- **Connections** — How the entity relates to other problems, features, and decisions in the graph. Each connection shows the relationship type, direction, and confidence score.

## Weekly brief

The weekly brief delivers a summary of problems, blind spots, and trends to a Slack channel on a recurring schedule.

<Note>
The weekly brief requires a connected Slack integration. If Slack is not connected, the brief section does not appear.
</Note>

### Set up the weekly brief

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Product Intelligence">
    Navigate to **Product Intelligence** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Set up">
    In the weekly brief section, click **Set up**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a Slack channel">
    Choose the channel where the brief should be posted. The Ravell bot must be invited to the channel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable">
    Click **Enable**. The brief sends every Monday at 9am UTC by default.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Configure the brief

After enabling, click the brief row to open editing. You can change:

- **Channel** — Which Slack channel receives the brief
- **Day** — Any weekday (Monday through Friday)
- **Time** — Hour of the day (00:00–23:00)
- **Timezone** — UTC, Europe/London, Europe/Dublin, US/Eastern, US/Central, US/Pacific, Asia/Tokyo, or Australia/Sydney

You can also **send a test** to preview the brief before the next scheduled run, or **pause** it to stop delivery temporarily.

## How problems are scored

Each problem receives a priority score based on:

- **Evidence count** — How many assertions mention it
- **Source diversity** — How many different source types reference it (e.g. Intercom + Linear + Slack scores higher than Slack alone)
- **Confidence** — Average confidence of the underlying assertions
- **Recency** — How recently the problem was last referenced

Problems with more evidence from more sources, with higher confidence and recent activity, rank higher.

---

## How blind spots are detected

A blind spot is an entity where Ravell sees signals that may not be getting enough attention. Each blind spot has:

- **Blind spot type** — The category of gap (e.g. rising mentions without a feature, unaddressed feedback)
- **Severity score** — How urgent the blind spot is, based on recent vs. baseline evidence
- **Source types** — Which integrations contributed to the detection
