## Overview

Workspace management covers members, invitations, and roles. Owners and (where permitted) members can manage these from **Settings**.

---

## Members

**Settings → Members** shows all workspace members with their roles (Owner, Admin, Member).

- **Change role**: Owners and admins can promote members to admin or demote admins to member.
- **Suspend**: Temporarily revoke access. The member can be unsuspended later.
- **Remove**: Permanently remove a member from the workspace.
- **Import from Slack**: When Slack is connected, admins can sync members from Slack to automatically import workspace members.

---

## Invitations

**Settings → Invitations** (or **Members → Invite**) lets you invite people by email. A badge appears on Invitations when you have pending invites to review.

- **Send invitation**: Enter email and assign a role (admin or member). The invitee receives an email with a link.
- **Pending invitations**: View and cancel pending invites.
- **Accept/Decline**: Invitees use the link in the email to accept or decline. Once accepted, they join with the assigned role.

---

## Roles

- **Owner**: Full access. Can manage members, billing, invitations, integrations, and delete the workspace.
- **Admin**: Can manage members, invitations, billing, and integrations. Cannot delete the workspace.
- **Member**: Can use Asks, Agents, Inbox, Sources. Cannot manage billing or remove other members.

Owners and admins can change roles and manage invitations.

## Team onboarding checklist

When onboarding your team to Ravell, follow this checklist:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set up the workspace">
    Create the workspace and connect your most-used sources (Linear, Intercom, Slack, Notion, Attio). See [Workspace setup](/workspace-setup).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete the workspace profile">
    Open **Context** and fill in the workspace profile: company mission, product context, goals, and ICP. This personalizes answers for everyone. See [Profiles](/profiles).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite your team">
    Go to **Settings → Members → Invite** and add teammates by email. Start with 2-3 people to pilot, then expand.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share a starter question">
    Send teammates a specific question to try first, like "What are the top open issues in our current cycle?" along with a link to the [Quickstart](/quickstart).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up a shared agent">
    Create a shared agent (e.g. **Weekly product roundup**) and deliver it to a team Slack channel. This gives passive value to people who haven't tried asking yet.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Workspace settings

**Settings → Workspace** (or **General**) lets you:

- Update workspace name.
- Set a company website URL (used to enrich your workspace profile with public company context).
- Complete onboarding (if applicable).
- Delete workspace (owners only).
