## What are Profiles?

**Profiles** describe who you are and what you work on. Ravell uses profiles to personalize answers — for example, prioritizing issues assigned to you or focusing on projects you care about.

**Why it matters:** Ravell learns your role, current work, and priorities from Linear, Slack, and past conversations. It understands your product, teams, and roadmap so answers are grounded in your reality, not generic. You get an answer immediately using cached profiles; refresh happens in the background. Answers feel tailored to you and your team from the first question.

There are two types:

- **Individual profile**: Your personal context (role, focus areas, projects).
- **Workspace profile**: Your team's context (mission, key projects, priorities).

---

## Individual profile

Your individual profile helps Ravell tailor answers to you. It includes:

- **Who you are**: Role (e.g. "Product Manager", "Engineer"), focus areas.
- **Preferences**: How you like answers formatted, level of detail.
- **Current work**: What you're actively working on.
- **Planned work**: Upcoming initiatives or projects.

Ravell uses this to surface relevant issues, conversations, and context when you ask questions.

---

## Workspace profile

The workspace profile describes your team. It includes:

- **Company context**: What the company does, market position.
- **Product context**: Main products or services.
- **Goals**: What the team is trying to achieve.
- **Team projects**: Important initiatives or products.
- **ICP (Ideal customer profile)**: Who you serve.
- **Operating context**: How the team works, constraints.

This helps Ravell understand context when answering questions that span the whole workspace.

---

## Refresh profiles

Update your profile when your role or focus changes. Use **Refresh** in Context to regenerate from your connected sources. Stale profiles lead to less relevant answers.

## Section overrides

When the auto-generated content for a profile section isn't quite right, you can override it with your own source material. Overrides work in two modes:

| Mode | What it does |
|------|-------------|
| **Enrichment** | Adds your source material as additional context alongside the AI-generated content |
| **Source of truth** | Replaces the AI-generated content entirely with content synthesized from your source |

### How to add an override

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the profile section">
    Go to **Context** and find the section you want to override (e.g. **Company context** on the workspace profile).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add an override">
    Click the override option for that section. Choose your source type:

    - **URL** — Provide a web URL. Ravell fetches and extracts the content.
    - **File** — Upload a PDF, Word document (.docx), or plain text file (max 5 MB).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the mode">
    Choose **Enrichment** to add context or **Source of truth** to replace the AI-generated content.
  </Step>
</Steps>

After you add or remove an override, Ravell automatically refreshes the profile section.

### Valid section keys

| Profile type | Sections you can override |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| **Individual** | Who you are, Preferences |
| **Workspace** | Company context, Product context, ICP, Goals, Operating context |

<Note>
  Each section can have only one **source of truth** override, but multiple **enrichment** overrides.
</Note>

## Source exclusions

Exclude specific URLs from being used when generating your profile. This is useful when a particular page adds noise or contains irrelevant information.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open exclusions">
    Go to **Context** and find the source exclusions setting for the profile type you want to adjust.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a URL">
    Enter the full URL (e.g. `https://example.com/page`) you want to exclude.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Exclusions are URL-based — you exclude individual pages, not entire integrations. You can set exclusions separately for your individual profile and the workspace profile.

## How profiles affect answers

When you ask a question, Ravell considers your individual profile, workspace profile, and connected sources together.

**Example**: Two people ask "What are the top issues?" — a PM on the checkout team sees checkout-related issues prioritized, while an engineering lead sees cross-team blocked issues. Same question, different context, different results.

### What makes a good profile

- **Role**: Be specific — "Product Manager, Payments team" is better than "PM"
- **Focus areas**: List 2-3 areas you work on most
- **Current work**: Update when you change projects so answers reflect your current context
- **Preferences**: Tell Ravell how you like answers — bullet points, executive summaries, technical detail

## Context used panel

When viewing an answer, the **Sources** rail may show a "Context used" panel. This displays which profile versions were used, freshness, and any quality flags. Quality flags indicate when profile data may need attention (e.g. stale or incomplete sections).
