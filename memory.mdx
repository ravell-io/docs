## What is Memory?

**Memory** is Ravell's store of what it learns about you over time. When you ask questions, have conversations, or share context, Ravell can remember key facts (e.g. "You work on the checkout team" or "You prefer summaries in bullet points"). This helps personalize future answers across sessions.

**Why it matters:** Facts you share are extracted and stored. Next time, they're already in context. For each question, Ravell pulls the most relevant memories; your role and identity are always included. The more you use Ravell, the better it understands you and the less you need to repeat yourself.

---

## What Memory stores

Each memory is a fact that Ravell uses to personalize your answers. Memories are organized into categories:

| Category | What it covers | Example |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| **Role and profile** | Your job title, team, and responsibilities | "I'm a PM on the checkout team" |
| **Projects** | What you're actively working on | "I'm leading the mobile redesign" |
| **Preferences** | How you like answers formatted | "I prefer bullet-point summaries" |
| **Product** | Facts about your product or features | "Our app supports iOS and Android" |
| **Internal workflow** | How your team works | "We use 2-week sprints starting Mondays" |
| **Company and business** | Company-level facts | "Our main competitor is Acme" |
| **Roadmap** | Upcoming plans and initiatives | "We're launching checkout v2 in Q3" |

Memory is used to improve relevance and avoid repeating information you've already provided.

---

## View and edit Memory

1. Go to **Settings → Memory**.
2. You'll see what Ravell has stored about you.
3. You can **edit** or **delete** individual items.
4. Use **Add** to explicitly tell Ravell something to remember.

---

## Clear Memory

If you want to start fresh:

1. Go to **Settings → Memory**.
2. Click **Clear memory**.
3. Confirm. Ravell will stop using stored facts for personalization until you provide new context.

Manually adding a few key facts makes a big difference. Here are some ideas:

| Memory | Why it helps |
|--------|-------------|
| "I work on the checkout team" | Scopes answers to your team's work |
| "I prefer bullet-point summaries" | Adjusts output format |
| "Our main competitor is [name]" | Improves competitive analysis |
| "I'm responsible for the mobile app" | Prioritizes mobile-related issues |
| "We use 2-week sprints starting on Mondays" | Aligns time-based questions with your cadence |

### Teaching Ravell your vocabulary

If your team uses specific terms, acronyms, or project names, add them as memories:

- "When I say 'Phoenix', I mean the checkout redesign project"
- "PDP means Product Detail Page"
- "CSM stands for Customer Success Manager"

This helps Ravell resolve references in your questions more accurately.

## How memories are created

Memories come from two sources:

- **Manually added** — When you add something in **Settings → Memory**, it's stored immediately. These are labeled as user-created.
- **Auto-extracted** — After a conversation, Ravell may extract key facts (e.g. your role, a project name, a preference you stated). Auto-extracted memories appear alongside manually added ones — you can edit or delete them at any time.

Ravell also tracks how often each memory is used in answers, so frequently relevant facts stay prominent.

---

## Verification and confidence

Each memory has a confidence level that reflects how certain Ravell is about the fact:

- **User-asserted** — You explicitly added or confirmed it. Highest confidence.
- **Corroborated** — Multiple sources or conversations support it.
- **Unverified** — Extracted from a single conversation. May need confirmation.
- **Contradicted** — A newer fact conflicts with this memory. Ravell flags it for your review.

You don't need to manage these manually — Ravell handles reconciliation automatically. If two memories conflict, the system flags the contradiction so you can resolve it.

---

## Control what gets stored

- **Toggle Memory on or off** — Go to **Settings → Memory** and use the toggle to enable or disable memory entirely. When off, Ravell won't store or use memories for personalization.
- **Explicit additions**: When you add something in Settings → Memory, it's stored.
- **Conversation context**: Ravell may extract facts from your questions and answers. You can edit or delete anything you don't want stored.
- **Privacy**: Memory is scoped to your account. Workspace members do not see your personal memory.
