## What is Memory?

**Memory** is Ravell's store of what it learns about you over time. When you ask questions, have conversations, or share context, Ravell can remember key facts (e.g. "You work on the checkout team" or "You prefer summaries in bullet points"). This helps personalize future answers across sessions.

**Why it matters:** Facts you share are extracted and stored. Next time, they're already in context. For each question, Ravell pulls the most relevant memories; your role and identity are always included. The more you use Ravell, the better it understands you and the less you need to repeat yourself.

---

## Enable or disable Memory

Memory is **disabled by default**. You need to turn it on before Ravell starts learning from your conversations.

1. Go to **Settings → Memory**.
2. Toggle **Memory** on to enable extraction.
3. When enabled, Ravell begins extracting facts from your conversations automatically.

You can turn Memory off at any time. When disabled, Ravell stops extracting new memories but keeps existing ones. Toggle it back on to resume.

---

## What Memory stores

Memories are organized into categories:

| Category | What it captures |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Role & profile** | Your role, title, team, and responsibilities |
| **Projects** | Projects you're working on or tracking |
| **Preferences** | How you like answers formatted, level of detail, communication style |
| **Product** | Product context, features, and domain knowledge you've shared |
| **Internal workflow** | Your processes, tools, and ways of working |
| **Company & business** | Company context, goals, strategy, and business information |
| **Roadmap** | Roadmap items, priorities, and planned work |

Each memory also tracks:

- **Source** — Whether it was auto-extracted from a conversation, manually created by you, or edited.
- **Confidence** — How certain Ravell is about the fact (0–100%). Memories below a confidence threshold are excluded from retrieval.
- **Verification status** — Unverified (new), user-asserted (you confirmed it), corroborated (supported by multiple conversations), or contradicted (conflicting evidence found). Contradicted memories are excluded from answers.

---

## How auto-extraction works

When Memory is enabled, Ravell analyzes your conversations and extracts relevant facts automatically. Not every conversation triggers extraction — Ravell looks for signals like:

- First-person statements about your role, preferences, or projects
- Workflow and process descriptions
- Product or business context you share
- Recurring topics and patterns

Extraction has a cooldown period to avoid over-extracting from rapid conversations. Strong signals (like explicit role statements) can bypass the cooldown. Each conversation can produce up to 5 memories.

---

## View and edit Memory

1. Go to **Settings → Memory**.
2. You'll see what Ravell has stored about you, organized by category.
3. You can **edit** or **delete** individual items.
4. Use **Add** to explicitly tell Ravell something to remember.

Manually added memories are marked as "user-created" and given higher confidence than auto-extracted ones.

---

## Clear Memory

If you want to start fresh:

1. Go to **Settings → Memory**.
2. Click **Clear memory**.
3. Confirm. Ravell will stop using stored facts for personalization until you provide new context.

## Suggested memories to add

Manually adding a few key facts makes a big difference. Here are some ideas:

| Memory | Why it helps |
|--------|-------------|
| "I work on the checkout team" | Scopes answers to your team's work |
| "I prefer bullet-point summaries" | Adjusts output format |
| "Our main competitor is [name]" | Improves competitive analysis |
| "I'm responsible for the mobile app" | Prioritizes mobile-related issues |
| "We use 2-week sprints starting on Mondays" | Aligns time-based questions with your cadence |

### Teaching Ravell your vocabulary

If your team uses specific terms, acronyms, or project names, add them as memories:

- "When I say 'Phoenix', I mean the checkout redesign project"
- "PDP means Product Detail Page"
- "CSM stands for Customer Success Manager"

This helps Ravell resolve references in your questions more accurately.

---

## Control what gets stored

- **Explicit additions**: When you add something in Settings → Memory, it's stored as user-created with high confidence.
- **Conversation context**: Ravell may infer facts from your questions and answers. You can delete anything you don't want stored.
- **Contradicted memories**: If Ravell finds conflicting information across conversations, it marks the memory as contradicted and excludes it from future answers.
- **Privacy**: Memory is scoped to your account. Workspace members do not see your personal memory.
