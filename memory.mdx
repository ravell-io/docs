## What is Memory?

**Memory** is Ravell's store of what it learns about you over time. When you ask questions, have conversations, or share context, Ravell can remember key facts (e.g. "You work on the checkout team" or "You prefer summaries in bullet points"). This helps personalize future answers across sessions.

**Why it matters:** Facts you share are extracted and stored. Next time, they're already in context. For each question, Ravell pulls the most relevant memories; your role and identity are always included. The more you use Ravell, the better it understands you and the less you need to repeat yourself.

---

## What Memory stores

- **Preferences**: How you like answers formatted, level of detail.
- **Context**: Facts you've shared (role, projects, team structure).
- **Patterns**: Recurring topics or sources you use often.

Memory is used to improve relevance and avoid repeating information you've already provided.

---

## How Memory learns automatically

Ravell doesn't just store what you explicitly add — it also learns from your conversations.

### Automatic extraction

After each conversation, Ravell analyzes the exchange and extracts up to five key facts worth remembering. Only high-confidence facts are stored (those above a minimum confidence threshold). Extracted memories are categorized automatically — for example, facts about your company go into "company/business", facts about your role go into "profile/role", and roadmap details are tagged as "roadmap".

### Web enrichment

When you mention a company, product, or concept in conversation, Ravell may search the web for additional context and synthesize extra memories from what it finds. This happens in the background so your next question benefits from richer context.

### Claim reconciliation

As memories accumulate, Ravell checks for contradictions and corroboration:

- **Corroboration**: When multiple conversations confirm the same fact, the memory's confidence increases.
- **Contradiction**: When new information conflicts with an existing memory (e.g. "We use 2-week sprints" vs. "We switched to 1-week sprints"), the older memory is down-weighted so the newer fact takes priority.

You can always override any memory by editing or deleting it manually.

---

## View and edit Memory

1. Go to **Settings → Memory**.
2. You'll see what Ravell has stored about you.
3. You can **edit** or **delete** individual items.
4. Use **Add** to explicitly tell Ravell something to remember.

---

## Clear Memory

If you want to start fresh:

1. Go to **Settings → Memory**.
2. Click **Clear memory**.
3. Confirm. Ravell will stop using stored facts for personalization until you provide new context.

## Suggested memories to add

Manually adding a few key facts makes a big difference. Here are some ideas:

| Memory | Why it helps |
|--------|-------------|
| "I work on the checkout team" | Scopes answers to your team's work |
| "I prefer bullet-point summaries" | Adjusts output format |
| "Our main competitor is [name]" | Improves competitive analysis |
| "I'm responsible for the mobile app" | Prioritizes mobile-related issues |
| "We use 2-week sprints starting on Mondays" | Aligns time-based questions with your cadence |

### Teaching Ravell your vocabulary

If your team uses specific terms, acronyms, or project names, add them as memories:

- "When I say 'Phoenix', I mean the checkout redesign project"
- "PDP means Product Detail Page"
- "CSM stands for Customer Success Manager"

This helps Ravell resolve references in your questions more accurately.

## How memories are selected

When you ask a question, Ravell doesn't use all your memories — it selects the most relevant ones using a scoring algorithm that considers:

- **Semantic similarity** to your question (weighted most heavily).
- **Importance** of the memory (high-importance facts like your role are prioritized).
- **Recency** (recently created or used memories score higher).
- **Confidence** (corroborated memories rank above unverified ones).
- **Usage frequency** (memories that have helped answer past questions get a small boost).

Memories about your role and identity are always included regardless of the question, so Ravell always knows who you are.

---

## Control what gets stored

- **Explicit additions**: When you add something in Settings → Memory, it's stored with the highest confidence.
- **Conversation context**: Ravell automatically infers facts from your questions and answers. You can delete anything you don't want stored.
- **Privacy**: Memory is scoped to your account. Workspace members do not see your personal memory.
