## Get started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in and create a workspace">
    Go to [app.ravell.io](https://app.ravell.io) and sign in with your email or Google. If you're joining an existing team, accept the workspace invitation from your email.

    A **workspace** is your team's shared space. If you're the first from your team, create a new workspace and give it a name. If you received an invitation, accept it to join an existing workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your internal tools">
    The onboarding flow prompts you to connect internal tools first. These give Ravell context on how your team works:

    - **Slack** — Conversations, decisions, and async updates
    - **Linear** — Issues, sprints, and project status
    - **Notion** — Docs, specs, and shared knowledge

    Click **Connect** for each tool and complete the OAuth flow in the popup. You must connect at least one internal source to continue.

    <Tip>
      The more context your agent has on how your team works, the better it understands your priorities.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect customer data sources">
    Next, connect tools where your customer data lives:

    - **Intercom** — Support tickets and customer conversations
    - **Attio** — CRM contacts and deal information
    - **Slack** — Customer-facing channels
    - **Linear** — Customer-reported bugs and feature requests
    - **Notion** — Customer feedback docs

    For Slack, Linear, and Notion you can select specific channels, teams, or pages to scope what Ravell indexes. This step is optional — you can skip it and add sources later in **Settings → Integrations**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask your first question">
    After completing onboarding, you land in the app. Click **New Ask** or use the main input:

    1. Type a question in plain language, e.g. "What did customers say about checkout last month?" or "What are the top open issues in our current cycle?"
    2. Press Enter. The answer streams in with citations you can click to see the source.
    3. Ask follow-up questions in the same thread for deeper context.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up your first agent">
    1. Open **Agents** in the sidebar and click **Create agent**.
    2. Pick a template — **Weekly product roundup** or **Daily standup prep** are great starting points.
    3. Customize the question and schedule (e.g. every Monday at 9am).
    4. Save. Click **Run now** to see the report immediately in your **Inbox**.

    <Tip>
      Enable **Deliver to Slack** to have reports posted to a channel automatically.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Using Ravell from Slack

Once Slack is connected, you can interact with Ravell directly in Slack:

- Type `/ravell` in any channel to add the Ravell agent — it picks up channel context automatically
- Mention `@Ravell` to ask questions or trigger actions without leaving Slack

See [Slack Agent](/slack-agent) for the full guide.
