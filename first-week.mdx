title: "Your first week with Ravell"

---

Get from zero to fully set up in five days. Each day takes 10-15 minutes.

---

## Day 1: Sign up and ask your first questions

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your workspace">
    Go to [app.ravell.io](https://app.ravell.io), sign in, and create a workspace for your team. See [Workspace setup](/workspace-setup) for details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your first source">
    Open **Sources** in the sidebar and connect Linear, Notion, Intercom, Slack, or Attio. Start with the tool your team uses most. Wait for the initial sync to complete.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask 3 questions">
    Click **New Ask** and try these:
    - "What are the top open issues in our current cycle?"
    - "What did customers say about [your product area] last month?"
    - "Summarize recent activity on [a project name]"

    Each answer comes with citations you can click to verify.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Day 2: Connect more sources and explore follow-ups

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect remaining sources">
    Go to **Sources** and connect any tools you haven't added yet. The more sources Ravell has, the richer your answers — especially for cross-tool questions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Try follow-up questions">
    Open one of yesterday's Asks and reply in the thread:
    - "Which of those are high priority?"
    - "Show me the Linear links for the top 3"
    - "What did we decide about that?"

    Ravell keeps full conversation context, so you don't need to repeat your original question.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Try scoping">
    Ask the same question scoped to different sources to see how answers change. Use the source selector or say "from Linear only" in your question. See [Scoping queries](/scoping-queries).
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Day 3: Set up your profile and workspace context

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review your individual profile">
    Open **Context** in the sidebar. Your profile is auto-generated from your connected sources. Review and edit sections like role, focus areas, and current work to improve personalization.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up the workspace profile">
    In **Context**, review the workspace profile. Add your company's mission, key projects, ICP, and goals. This helps Ravell align answers with your team's priorities.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a few memories">
    Go to **Settings → Memory** and add facts Ravell should always know about you:
    - "I work on the checkout team"
    - "I prefer bullet-point summaries"
    - "Our main competitor is [name]"
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Day 4: Create your first agent

<Steps>
  <Step title="Browse templates">
    Open **Agents** in the sidebar and click **Create agent**. Browse the template gallery — there are templates for weekly roundups, standup prep, churn signals, support trends, and more. See [Agent templates](/agent-templates).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a template and customize">
    Start with a template like **Weekly product roundup** or **Daily standup prep**. Edit the question to match your team's needs. Choose a schedule (e.g. every Monday at 9am).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run it now">
    Click **Run now** to see the report immediately. Check your **Inbox** for the result. Open the report to read it and try following up with questions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Day 5: Install the Slack Agent and try actions

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set up the Slack Agent">
    If Slack is connected, the Slack Agent is ready. Mention **@Ravell** in any channel or DM, or use `/ravell`. See [Slack Agent](/slack-agent).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask a question from Slack">
    Try: `@Ravell What are the top issues in our current sprint?`

    Ravell responds in-thread with an answer and citations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Try a Linear action">
    Try: `@Ravell Create a bug about login timeouts` or `@Ravell Update ENG-123 to done`.

    You can create issues, update statuses, and manage projects without leaving Slack.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable Slack delivery for your agent">
    Edit the agent you created on Day 4. Set visibility to **Shared**, enable **Deliver to Slack**, and pick a channel. Your team gets proactive reports right in Slack.
  </Step>
</Steps>
