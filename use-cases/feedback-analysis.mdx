Customer feedback is scattered across Intercom conversations, Slack channels, and Attio notes. Ravell brings it all together and identifies themes so you can act on patterns, not individual messages.

---

## The question

> "Cluster customer feedback about [topic] from the last 30 days"

Replace `[topic]` with your area of interest: onboarding, pricing, mobile app, API, checkout, or leave it broad for a general overview.

---

## What you get

Ravell searches Intercom, Slack, and Attio for customer feedback, then groups it by theme:

- **Theme clusters**: Grouped feedback with counts (e.g. "Confusing setup flow — 12 mentions")
- **Sentiment signals**: Whether feedback is positive, negative, or neutral
- **Representative quotes**: Specific customer statements for each theme, with citations
- **Cross-source coverage**: Feedback from Intercom conversations, Slack discussions, and Attio notes combined

---

## Example workflows

### Feature-specific feedback

**Ask**: "What did customers say about our new pricing page?"

**Follow-up**: "Which of those are from enterprise accounts?"

**Follow-up**: "Create a Linear issue for the top complaint"

### Trend tracking

**Ask**: "How has customer sentiment about onboarding changed over the last 3 months?"

**Follow-up**: "What did we ship that might have affected sentiment?"

### Competitive analysis

**Ask**: "What competitors did customers mention in Intercom conversations this month?"

**Follow-up**: "What features are they comparing?"

---

## Automate it with an agent

Set up a **Support trends** agent to run every Monday:

> "Summarize the top themes from customer conversations this week. Include volume, sentiment, and representative quotes."

Deliver to your product or CS Slack channel so the team stays informed without manual effort.

---

## Tips

- **Time ranges improve focus**: "Last 30 days" catches trends. "This week" catches recent spikes.
- **Cross-source is powerful**: Ask "from all sources" to see if internal Slack discussions match customer feedback.
- **Follow up with action**: After identifying a theme, use "Create a Linear issue for [theme]" to turn insight into work.
