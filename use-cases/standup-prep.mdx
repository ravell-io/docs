Instead of manually checking Linear, Slack, and Intercom before standup, let Ravell compile everything for you.

---

## The question

Ask this before your standup:

> "What happened in the backend team since yesterday? Focus on completed work, new issues, and blockers."

Customize for your team: replace "backend team" with your team name, and adjust the time range to match your standup cadence.

---

## What you get

Ravell searches Linear for status changes, new issues, and blocked items. If Slack is connected, it includes relevant discussions from team channels. The answer includes:

- **Completed work**: Issues moved to done since the last standup
- **New issues**: Issues created or newly assigned
- **Blockers**: Items flagged as blocked with context on why
- **Key discussions**: Relevant Slack threads from team channels

Each item includes citations so you can click through to the source.

---

## Automate it with an agent

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the agent">
    Open **Agents** → **Create agent** → select the **Daily standup prep** template.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customize the question">
    Edit the question to match your team. Be specific about what you want: completions, blockers, new work, or all three.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the schedule">
    Schedule it for 15-30 minutes before your standup (e.g. every weekday at 8:30am).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable Slack delivery">
    Set visibility to **Shared** and enable **Deliver to Slack**. Choose your team's standup channel. The report appears automatically before your meeting.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips

- **Scope by team**: Always include your team name for focused results.
- **Time ranges matter**: "Since yesterday" gives you standup-relevant recency. "This week" gives a broader view.
- **Follow up in thread**: After reading the report, ask "Tell me more about [blocker]" for context.
