A weekly product roundup keeps the entire team informed about what shipped, what customers are saying, and what's coming next. Automate it with Ravell instead of compiling it manually.

---

## The question

> "Create a weekly product roundup: what shipped this week, top customer feedback, upcoming priorities, and any open blockers"

---

## What you get

A comprehensive weekly digest combining data from all your sources:

- **What shipped**: Completed Linear issues grouped by team or project
- **Customer feedback**: Top themes from Intercom and Slack
- **Upcoming priorities**: High-priority issues for next week
- **Blockers**: Items flagged as blocked with context

---

## Set it up as an agent

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create from template">
    Open **Agents** → **Create agent** → select **Weekly product roundup**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customize">
    Edit the question to match what your team cares about. Add specific teams, projects, or topics.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Schedule">
    Set it to run every Friday at 4pm (end-of-week digest) or Monday at 8am (start-of-week context).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Deliver to Slack">
    Set visibility to **Shared** and enable **Deliver to Slack**. Choose a channel like #product or #general.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Push to Notion (optional)">
    After each run, open the report and click **Push to Notion** to archive it in a Notion database for long-term records.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Variations

| Audience | Question variation |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Leadership** | "Executive summary: what shipped, key metrics, top risks" |
| **Engineering** | "Engineering weekly: completed issues, open bugs, tech debt items" |
| **Product** | "Product weekly: shipped features, customer reactions, roadmap updates" |
| **Whole company** | "Company weekly: cross-functional highlights and customer wins" |

---

## Tips

- **Experiment with questions**: Run the agent manually with different questions to find the format your team likes best.
- **Multiple roundups**: Create separate agents for different audiences (leadership vs engineering vs whole team).
- **Archive in Notion**: Push weekly reports to a Notion database to build a searchable history of product progress.
