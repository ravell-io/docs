A good sprint review needs three things: what shipped, what didn't, and how customers reacted. Ravell compiles all three from your connected tools.

---

## The question

> "Summarize our sprint: what shipped, what slipped, and what customers said about the features we shipped"

Customize with your team name and cycle name for more focused results.

---

## What you get

Ravell produces a structured review:

- **Shipped**: Completed Linear issues with links, grouped by project or team
- **Slipped**: Incomplete issues that were planned for this cycle, with reasons when available
- **Customer reaction**: Intercom and Slack feedback about recently shipped features
- **Key metrics**: Issue counts, completion rates, rollover items

---

## Step-by-step workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Before the meeting: Run the question">
    Ask the question above 30 minutes before your sprint review. Or set up an agent to run it automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the report">
    Open the report in your Inbox or the Ask thread. Click citations to verify any claims you want to discuss.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Dig deeper in follow-ups">
    - "Which slipped issues are highest priority?"
    - "What blocked the items that didn't ship?"
    - "Did any customers mention the features we shipped?"
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share with the team">
    Push the report to Notion for documentation, or deliver it to a Slack channel for async review.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Automate it

Create an agent with the **Weekly product roundup** template. Schedule it for the day before your sprint review. Set visibility to **Shared** and enable **Deliver to Slack** so the team can read it before the meeting.

---

## Example questions for sprint reviews

- "Compare what we planned vs what we shipped this cycle"
- "Which issues rolled over from last sprint?"
- "What's the bug-to-feature ratio this sprint?"
- "Show me customer feedback about features released this week"
- "What decisions were made in #engineering this sprint?"
