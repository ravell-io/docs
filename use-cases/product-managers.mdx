As a PM, you spend hours gathering context from Linear, Intercom, Slack, and Notion. Ravell gives you evidence-backed answers in seconds so you can focus on decisions, not data collection.

---

## Top workflows

### Sprint summaries

Get a complete picture of what shipped, what slipped, and what customers said about shipped features.

**Ask**: "Summarize what the Payments team shipped this sprint and what customers said about those features"

Ravell searches Linear for completed issues, cross-references with Intercom feedback, and produces a single summary with citations.

**Suggested agent**: Set up a **Weekly product roundup** agent every Friday at 4pm to automate this.

---

### Customer feedback clustering

Understand what customers are actually saying without reading every conversation.

**Ask**: "Cluster customer feedback from Intercom about onboarding in the last 30 days"

Ravell groups feedback by theme (e.g. "confusing setup flow", "missing documentation", "slow first value") and shows how many conversations mention each theme.

**Follow-up**: "Which of those themes are addressed by our current roadmap?"

---

### Roadmap alignment

Check if what you're building matches what customers need.

**Ask**: "How does our current Linear roadmap compare to customer requests from Intercom?"

Ravell identifies gaps between planned features and customer demand, highlighting requests that don't map to any planned work.

**Suggested agent**: Set up a **Roadmap vs reality** agent weekly to track alignment over time.

---

### Feature request triage

Quickly triage incoming feature requests with full context.

**Ask**: "What feature requests came in from enterprise customers this week?"

**Follow-up**: "Which of those already have Linear issues?"

**Follow-up**: "Create a Linear issue for the top request that doesn't have one"

---

### Stakeholder reports

Generate reports and push them to Notion for sharing.

**Ask**: "Create a one-page stakeholder summary of this week's product progress"

**Follow-up**: "Push this to Notion in the Weekly Reports database"

## Suggested agents

| Template | Schedule | What you get |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| **Weekly product roundup** | Every Friday | What shipped, top issues, customer feedback summary |
| **Feature clustering** | Every Monday | Grouped feature requests from the past week |
| **Priorities gap** | Every Monday | Mismatches between priorities and active work |
| **Roadmap vs reality** | Bi-weekly | Customer demand vs planned features |

---

## Example questions

- "What are the top 5 feature requests this month?"
- "Summarize feedback about [feature] from all sources"
- "What did we ship vs what we planned this cycle?"
- "Which issues have been deprioritized more than twice?"
- "Create a summary of the current cycle's progress for the team"

---

## Getting started

1. Connect **Linear** and **Intercom** (minimum). Add Slack and Notion for richer context.
2. Set up your [individual profile](/profiles) with your role and focus areas.
3. Create a **Weekly product roundup** agent from the [template gallery](/agent-templates).
4. Try the questions above and build follow-up workflows.
