## Overview

Connect **Notion** to Ravell to search across your Notion workspace. Ravell indexes pages and databases so you can ask questions like "What did we document about the checkout flow?" and get answers with citations. You can also **take actions in Notion** — push reports, create pages, build databases, and more — from Web Asks, the Slack Agent, and Inbox.

---

## Connect Notion

1. Go to **Sources** or **Settings → Integrations**.
2. Click **Connect** next to Notion.
3. Sign in to Notion and authorize Ravell.
4. Select which pages or databases to sync (or sync the whole workspace).
5. Ravell starts syncing. Initial sync may take a few minutes for large workspaces.

---

## What you can ask

| Question | What Ravell does |
|----------|------------------|
| "What did we document about the checkout flow?" | Searches Notion pages for checkout-related content |
| "Find the product roadmap page" | Direct lookup of a specific document |
| "Summarize our onboarding documentation" | Synthesizes content from onboarding-related pages |
| "What specs were written last month?" | Finds recently created or updated spec documents |
| "What's in our competitive analysis database?" | Searches database entries for competitive content |
| "Find all pages mentioning [feature name]" | Full-text and semantic search across your Notion workspace |

---

## What gets synced

- **Pages**: Title, content, properties.
- **Databases**: Schema and entries.

Ravell uses this data to answer questions and provide citations back to Notion pages.

## Push to Notion

Push any Ask answer or Agent report to Notion as a new page:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the answer">
    Open an Ask or Inbox report with the content you want to push.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Push to Notion">
    Click **Push to Notion** on the answer card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose destination">
    Select the parent page or database where the new page should go.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Ravell creates the page with the report content and citations, formatted for Notion (headings, lists, links).
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Use cases for Push to Notion:**
- Share answers with your team as Notion docs
- Build a knowledge base of recurring agent reports
- Archive sprint reviews and weekly roundups
- Create meeting prep docs from Ravell answers

## Notion actions (natural language)

Beyond pushing reports, you can take a wide range of actions in Notion using natural language — from Web Ask follow-ups or the Slack Agent. Just describe what you want and Ravell handles the rest.

### Create pages

Create new Notion pages in a database or under an existing page:

- "Create a Notion page summarizing this week's customer feedback"
- "Add a page called 'Sprint 42 review' to the Product database"
- "Create a meeting notes page under the Q2 Planning page"

Pages are created with formatted markdown content (headings, lists, links).

### Update pages

Update existing Notion page titles or archive pages you no longer need:

- "Rename that Notion page to 'Final API spec'"
- "Archive the old onboarding page"

### Append content to pages

Add content blocks to an existing page without replacing what's already there:

- "Add the key takeaways to the sprint review page"
- "Append this summary to the product roadmap page"

### Add comments

Comment on Notion pages to leave notes or context:

- "Add a comment on the roadmap page about the timeline change"

### Create and update databases

Create new databases with custom property schemas, or update existing ones:

- "Create a database called 'Feature Requests' with Status, Priority, and Owner columns"
- "Add a 'Due Date' property to the Tasks database"

### Search Notion

Ask Ravell to search your Notion workspace directly:

- "Find the product roadmap in Notion"
- "Search Notion for pages about onboarding"

### Set a default database

You can set a personal **default database** for Notion exports. Once set, Ravell uses it as the default destination when you push reports or create pages without specifying where.

- "Set the Product Reports database as my default"

## Re-sync and reindex

Ravell syncs Notion data periodically. Trigger a manual **Reindex** or **Sync** from **Sources** to refresh data after adding new pages or updating content.
