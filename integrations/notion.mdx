## Overview

Connect **Notion** to Ravell to search across your Notion workspace. Ravell indexes pages and databases so you can ask questions like "What did we document about the checkout flow?" and get answers with citations. You can also **push reports to Notion**, create and update pages, and manage databases — from the web app or Slack.

---

## Connect Notion

1. Go to **Sources** or **Settings → Integrations**.
2. Click **Connect** next to Notion.
3. Sign in to Notion and authorize Ravell.
4. Select which pages or databases to sync (or sync the whole workspace).
5. Ravell starts syncing. Initial sync may take a few minutes for large workspaces.

---

## What you can ask

| Question | What Ravell does |
|----------|------------------|
| "What did we document about the checkout flow?" | Searches Notion pages for checkout-related content |
| "Find the product roadmap page" | Direct lookup of a specific document |
| "Summarize our onboarding documentation" | Synthesizes content from onboarding-related pages |
| "What specs were written last month?" | Finds recently created or updated spec documents |
| "What's in our competitive analysis database?" | Searches database entries for competitive content |
| "Find all pages mentioning [feature name]" | Full-text and semantic search across your Notion workspace |

---

## What gets synced

- **Pages**: Title, content, properties.
- **Databases**: Schema and entries.

Ravell uses this data to answer questions and provide citations back to Notion pages.

## Push to Notion

Push any Ask answer or Agent report to Notion as a new page:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the answer">
    Open an Ask or Inbox report with the content you want to push.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Push to Notion">
    Click **Push to Notion** on the answer card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose destination">
    Select the parent page or database where the new page should go.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Ravell creates the page with the report content and citations, formatted for Notion (headings, lists, links).
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Use cases for Push to Notion:**
- Share answers with your team as Notion docs
- Build a knowledge base of recurring agent reports
- Archive sprint reviews and weekly roundups
- Create meeting prep docs from Ravell answers

## Notion actions

Beyond Push to Notion, you can take a range of actions in Notion using natural language — from both the web app and Slack.

### What you can do

| Action | Example prompt |
|--------|---------------|
| **Create a page** | "Create a Notion page summarizing this week's feedback" |
| **Update a page** | "Update the sprint review page with this week's results" |
| **Append content** | "Add a section to the product roadmap about the auth feature" |
| **Add a comment** | "Add a comment on the checkout spec page" |
| **Create a database** | "Create a Notion database called Weekly Reports" |
| **Update a database** | "Update the Weekly Reports database schema" |
| **Set default database** | "Set my default Notion database to Product Reports" |

### How it works

When you ask Ravell to take a Notion action (in a follow-up or from Slack), Ravell delegates to a dedicated Notion sub-agent that:

1. Searches your connected Notion workspace to find the right page or database.
2. Performs the action (create, update, or append).
3. Formats content with proper headings, lists, and links.
4. Reports back with a link to the created or updated page.

<Tip>
Set a **default Notion database** so "Push this to Notion" always goes to the right place. Ask Ravell: "Set my default Notion database to [database name]".
</Tip>

### From the web app

In any Ask thread, ask Ravell to push content or create Notion pages as a follow-up:

- "Push this to Notion"
- "Create a Notion page with these findings"
- "Update the roadmap page with this analysis"

### From Slack

Mention **@Ravell** with your Notion request. See the full list in the [Slack Agent command reference](/slack-agent#command-reference).

## Re-sync and reindex

Ravell syncs Notion data periodically. Trigger a manual **Reindex** or **Sync** from **Sources** to refresh data after adding new pages or updating content.
